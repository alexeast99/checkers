What is Checkers?

Checkers is a board game played between two people on an 8x8 checked board. The pieces of checkers are called "men". There are two types of pieces, denoting each player's team; red and black. Each player has 12 men, which are initially setup on the first three rows of two opposite sides of the board, placed on every other dark square.

How to Play

Pieces are always moved diagonally. A piece can move diagonally forward over another piece if the latter piece has a free square directly diagonal to itself. In this situation, the piece that gets "jumped" will be removed from the board and no longer usable. A chain of "jumps" is possible if there are multiple pieces spaced diagonally with one space in between each (a sequence of single jumps). In the event that a piece makes it to the side of the board opposite of where it originated, that piece becomes a "king" and can now move in any diagonal direction at any time. A player wins the game when they eliminate all of their opponents pieces.

Other Rules

* If a player has an opportunity to jump, they must do so.

* The darker colored pieces move first.
